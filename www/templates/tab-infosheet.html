Participant Information Sheet

My Mood Minder: A mobile phone application for people receiving treatment for depression

What is the purpose of this study?

Previous research has found that people do not always stick to their anti-depressant treatment plan. The research team have designed a mobile phone application (app) in collaboration with people with depression to help with the depression treatment plan. This study aims to get feedback about whether or not the mobile phone app is helpful to people who are receiving treatment for depression. The app has two main functions. 1. The app will send you daily texts which can be chosen by you to suit your needs. The texts may contain encouraging messages or information which might help people with depression. 2. The other function is a symptom tracker. This will involve filling in a weekly questionnaire (symptom record) about the symptoms of depression and the app will allow you to review it whenever you like.

Why have I been invited?

You have been invited to the study because you have been prescribed anti-depressant therapy for an episode of depression.

Do I have to take part?

No, taking part is voluntary. If you would prefer not to take part you do not have to give a reason. You are free to withdraw at any time and without giving a reason. If you take part, but later change your mind, you can withdraw from the study at any time. Any current or future healthcare that you receive will not be affected by deciding whether or not to take part in the study.

What will happen if I take part?

If you take part, you will be invited by phone to attend your GP surgery for an audio-recorded interview with a researcher about what your expectations are about the app and what experience you have with mobile phone technology. You do not need to have any previous experience with apps as you will be shown how to use it at the interview. If you do not wish to put the app on your mobile phone, or if your phone is not suitable for the app, the study will provide you with a suitable phone at no charge to you for a duration of six weeks for the purpose of the study only. We will phone you after three weeks in case there is anything you wish to discuss during the study. After six weeks of using the app, we will interview you a second time about your experience. It should be noted that the app is not intended to replace any contact you already have with health staff or services nor does it provide a crisis or emergency service.

What do I have to do if I choose to take part?

If you wish to take part, please return the reply slip using the prepaid envelope. We will then contact you and arrange a suitable time for the first interview. Before you begin the interview and are introduced to using the app, you will be asked to sign a consent form which indicates you have read this information sheet and that you are willing to take part in the study. We will then ask you to answer some basic questions about your background details and fill in two very short questionnaires (approximately five minutes) about taking anti-depressant medication). After the questionnaires, we will show you how to use the app so that you receive regular texts which are relevant to you, and so you know how to track your symptoms. This will then be followed by an audio-recorded interview about your expectations of using the app. The visit will take approximately one hour. For the next six weeks you are free to use the app as little or as much as you wish and you can change the configuration. There is technical help by phone on request if needed for the app. You will then be invited to take part in a second audio-recorded interview at the GP practice which will involve short questionnaires and speaking about the experience of using the app.

Expenses and payments

Travel expenses to and from the interview at the GP surgery can be reimbursed by the research team. Any costs of data, phone calls or texts made using the app will also be refunded. There are no expenses or costs in taking part to the participant.

What are the possible benefits of taking part?

There are no direct benefits to taking part in the study. If you take part, the information you provide will help to inform the research community to improve future treatment to patients with depression.

What are the possible risks of taking part?

Some of the questions may be considered personal or sensitive and answering the questions or receiving texts may stir up feelings. Some useful contacts have been provided at the end of this information sheet in case you would like to speak to somebody about difficult feelings which may arise during the period of the study. The useful contacts can also be viewed in the app in the help section.

Will my taking part be kept confidential?

Your information will be stored confidentially and anonymously. All participant information is stored on password protected computer databases and in locked filing cabinets and will only be accessible to the University of Aberdeen research team and regulatory authorities for auditing and monitoring purposes. You will be allocated a unique study number and staff not directly involved with you will know you only by this number. When the results of the study are reported, individuals who have taken part will not be identified in any way. The interview will not be replayed to anybody outside the research team unless for auditing and monitoring purpose by regulatory authorities. Anonymised direct quotes from your interview may be published in the study’s findings. Anonymised data may also be shared with researchers from other academic institutions

What if I change my mind about taking part?

If you decide to withdraw from the study, your standard of care will not be affected. You will still be asked to attend the routine follow-up appointments required as part of your standard care. These follow up appointments with your doctor or health service provider will not be part of the study. If you withdraw from the study, all information that we have obtained up to the point of you coming out of the study will continue to be used for the purpose of the study, unless you indicate otherwise.

What if there is a problem?

If you wish to complain about any aspect of the way in which you have been approached or treated during the course of this study, you should contact Dr Isobel Cameron on 01224 557966. The University has arrangements in place to provide for harm arising from participation in the study for which the University is the Research Sponsor.

Will my GP be informed of my involvement in the study?

Yes. We will send your GP a brief letter informing them of your participation in the study.

What will happen to any information I give?

The University complies with the Data Protection Act and all information will be treated with the strictest confidence. The interviews which are recorded with be sent to a transcription service with no identifiable information as we will use your unique study number. The results of the questionnaires will be entered anonymously into a data spreadsheet. Anonymised data may be shared with others for research purposes. The data will be analysed by the research team at the University of Aberdeen. If any of the information is needed for future studies, permission must be obtained from a Research Ethics Committee, and the data will remain anonymous.

How will the information I provide be used?

We plan to publish the results in a health journal so others can read about it and learn from the results of the study.

Funding

The research is funded by the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council.

Approval

This research has ethical approval from the North East of Scotland Research Ethics Committee, Research and Development and NHS Grampian.

If the phone is lost or stolen

If the phone is lost or stolen, we would appreciate if you could contact us (see below for Study Contacts) and let us know so we can cancel the data package. Alternatively we will be in touch after three weeks for a mid-study check up call and towards the end of the six weeks to confirm the time of the interview, and you can report it to us then.
